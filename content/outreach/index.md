Need an astronomy expert? I do frequent outreach work in schools, pubs, on the radio, and occasionally even on TV. 

I am a passionate advocate for underrepresented groups in science. In my capacity as a queer disabled woman, I believe it is important to be visible and to share my experiences so that young people from similar backgrounds can see that a career in science is possible. In recognition of this I was awarded a Queensland Museum award for Breaking Barriers in STEM. 

The image on the right is from a Wear it Purple Day event at the University of Sydney, where I was part of a panel of queer STEM professionals giving career advice. I was also part of their [Breaking Barriers series](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/usydcc_careerscentre-breakingbarriers-usydalumni-ugcPost-7388703359308001280-9RI8?utm_source=social_share_send&utm_medium=member_desktop_web&rcm=ACoAACps1oEBPjknNxuLfpNjgA2ntFIgs8u275E). A social media series that spotlights USyd alumni from underrepresented backgrounds.

Shoot me an [email](mailto:rebecca.mcelroy@unisq.edu.au) or reach out on [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/breaky.bsky.social) or [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rebecca-mcelroy/) and we can see what we can organise. I also have several fantastic students who love outreach and would love to be involved.
